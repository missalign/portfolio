Hong Kong Immersion

Facilitated a 2-week deep-dive immersive design journey for 20 Coca-Cola worldwide senior executives to explore plastic bottle consumption, waste behaviors, and circular ecosystem interventions in Hong Kong.

Context

Commissioned by the Coca-Cola Global Office (Atlanta/US) and conducted by MJV Technology & Innovation, the Hong Kong Immersion placed 20 global high executives on the ground for 2 weeks. The goal was to run a rapid design-led exploration into the realities of plastic and PET recycling within an urban metropolis facing extreme space constraints and critical waste management issues.


Challenge

The Macro Environment

  • Worst-Case Waste Scenario: Studying a high-density environment facing severe trash overproduction and limited processing infrastructure.
  • Lack of Space: Navigating infrastructure limitations where physical space for storing and sorting recyclables inside residences and businesses is scarce.
  • Cultural Ecosystem Disconnection: Bridging the gap between corporate sustainability targets and actual local user behavior on the streets.

    • Executive Learning Alignment

  • Immersive Contextualization: Getting global executives out of headquarters and directly onto the streets to build raw, local empathy.
  • Stakeholder Skepticism: Overcoming deep social stigmas tied to manual recycling and physical material collectors in urban communities.
  • Actionable Scalability: Transforming unstructured field observations into concrete corporate initiatives and investment frameworks.

    • Discovery & Research

    Key Field Discoveries:

  • Inconvenience & Scale: Recycling options are highly inconvenient, and there is too little physical space available.
  • Trust & Functionality: A prevalent lack of trust in tap water makes PET-packaged beverages highly desirable for consumption "on the go".
  • Perceptions & Visibility: There is low visibility surrounding recycling processes, and a very low financial value is perceived on PET material.
  • Social Factors: While parents actively want to set good examples for children, a social stigma persists around collecting waste materials.
  • Plurality of Value: Multiple value vectors coexist across different groups—spanning financial reward, social recognition, goodwill, and regulations.

    • Point of View

    The field research was systematically categorized into 7 core "How Might We" pillars to transition findings from pure analysis straight into iterative ideation:

    Impact & Systemic Solutions

    The ultimate delivery transformed systemic Friction Points across the recycling journey into structural Value Solutions:

    The Consumer & Sorter Touchpoints

  • Consumer Focus: Moved users from being confused/not knowing how to "doing good" through intuitive design and targeted education.
  • Sorter Focus: Overcame physical convenience and availability roadblocks through optimized, better placement of collection bins.

    • The Value Chain Reframe

  • Collector Focus: Reframed the social stigma and low-value association by honoring collectors as the "Heroes" of the ecosystem.
  • Buyer / Reseller Focus: Eliminated arbitrary pricing uncertainty by debunking trade myths and establishing clear, set pricing models.

    • Industrial Infrastructure

  • Recycling & Transformation: Solved processing deficits ("No Plant") by integrating direct infrastructure loops like a dedicated Swire processing plant to extract hidden residual value.

    • Reflection

  • Value is Not Multi-Genre: Perceptions of waste are tied heavily to context. To get high-end consumers to recycle, the loop must prioritize social recognition or design convenience; for street collectors, the priority is optimization, tools, and dignity.
  • Reframe Stigmas through Service Design: Instead of hiding structural waste collectors, treating them explicitly as "Heroes" within the service blueprinted journey creates unexpected touchpoints for positive civic reinforcement.
  • Corporate Empathy Sparks Action: Immersing global executives directly in a hyper-dense trash environment forces immediate design constraints, cutting out standard corporate bureaucracy to spawn 100 highly practical ideas in just days.
