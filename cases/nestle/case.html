U4HK / Nutrir Project

Designed a unified social innovation framework and systemic service ecosystem that reorganizes corporate social investments to encourage healthy habits in children through co-creation, physical tools, and community-led solutions.

Context

Developed by Agência Tellus in partnership with Nestlé and the Nestlé Foundation, this strategic design project focused on optimizing the operations and target synergy between two distinct nutritional social programs. Spanning a deep dive into municipal school networks, the initiative aimed to encourage children aged 5 to 9 to sustainably adopt healthier habits, including balanced eating, water intake, and physical activity.


Challenge

Strategic & Operational

  • Parallel program tracks increasing overall corporate operational costs.
  • Fragmented external communications with municipal departments and public actors.
  • Coverage gaps where target audiences were only being reached by one of the two initiatives.
  • Bridging the corporate social investment gap to create a unified public-private service model.

    • Target Audience & Network

  • Traditional top-down corporate health messages failing to permanently alter child habits.
  • Information asymmetry between corporate planning teams and local school field execution.
  • Designing engaging, low-tech interventions for families with limited digital or internet access.
  • Activating the complex constellation of surrounding influencers (families, teachers, cooks, and local managers).

    • Discovery & Research

    Core Service Principles Matrix:

  • The Child as a Network: Treat the child not as an isolated individual, but as the center of several structural ripples of influence.
  • Pillars of Human-Centered Design: Drive all continuous iteration using the three core pillars of Empathy, Cocreation, and Experimentation.
  • Tri-Partite Capacity Mapping: Activate every ecosystem archetype across three clear roles: Care Giver, Role Model, and Teacher.
  • Creating Shared Value: Transition philanthropic efforts away from simple field execution into sustainable, public-policy induction models.

    • Design Exploration & Delivery

    The system resulted in 29 conceptual solutions, which were refined into a series of actionable service blueprints and field-tested prototypes designed to bridge operational gaps and maximize community habit adoption. A foundational solution implemented was a computer game to incentivize children to adopt healthier habits; based on their real-world activities (measured by a smart tracking bracelet), their computer game character would receive bonus stats and progression rewards.

    Impact

    Direct Service Scope

  • 14 Schools Engaged: Deep field research and ethnographic immersion conducted across four distinct Brazilian municipalities.
  • 100+ Stakeholders Co-creating: Directly integrated over 100 children alongside teachers, school cooks, directors, and municipal secretaries into the design process.

    • Social Innovation

  • Minha Legião: Successfully tested an offline comic-book and challenge-card game to bypass digital exclusion in segments lacking connectivity.
  • Unidos a Você Platform: Gathered 44 civic-led health ideas in an open innovation pilot, culminating in a community-wide activation weekend.

    • Infrastructure Assets

  • Caixa-Guia Toolboxes: Distributed targeted physical kits and action roadmaps to harmonize execution plans between city halls and educators.
  • Strategic Restructuring Blueprints: Delivered unified operational reorganization grids and 2017 blueprints to merge corporate and foundation assets.

    • Reflection

  • Schools are Irreplaceable Operations: While peer-led stimuli work well at home, direct-mail distribution suffered massive drops; physical school networks remain vital operational anchors.
  • Play Early with Municipalities: Initiating local city negotiations 4 to 6 months prior to deployment is required to successfully align with technical school calendars before they lock.
  • Move Toward Advocacy over Execution: True Creating Shared Value (CSV) is unlocked when a corporate foundation pivots from basic field operations into a high-level vehicle for national public policy induction.
